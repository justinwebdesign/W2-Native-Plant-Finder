Sagebrush (Artemisia tridentata)

Summary: Iconic shrub of sagebrush steppe; silver-gray foliage reduces water loss.

Care: Prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. Full sun. Drought-tolerant once established. No summer watering needed.

Insects: Primarily wind-pollinated; supports insect and bird habitat.

Blooming: Late summer to fall

Best Planting Time: Fall preferred; spring also suitable

Gray Rabbitbrush (Ericameria nauseosa)

Summary: Bright yellow blooms late summer; attracts pollinators.

Care: Adaptable to sandy and rocky soils. Full sun. Drought-tolerant. No irrigation needed once established.

Insects: Wide variety of insects (such as beetles and wasps); crucial resource in late season.

Blooming: Late summer to fall

Best Planting Time: Fall

Bitterbrush (Purshia tridentata)

Summary: Dry slope native; key forage species for wildlife.

Care: Grows well in rocky, well-drained soils. Requires full sun and no watering after establishment.

Insects: Host plant for Brown Elfin butterfly (Callophrys augustinus).

Blooming: Spring (April to June)

Best Planting Time: Fall or early spring

Oregon Sunshine (Eriophyllum lanatum)

Summary: Bright yellow perennial; drought-tolerant once established.

Care: Dry, rocky or sandy soils. Full sun. Water only during extreme drought.

Insects: Bees, flies, and butterflies.

Blooming: Spring to early summer

Best Planting Time: Fall preferred

Purple Sage (Salvia dorrii)

Summary: Compact shrub with purple blooms; great for dry gardens.

Care: Dry, well-drained sandy soils. Full sun. Minimal watering once established.

Insects: Bees and hummingbirds.

Blooming: Late spring to early summer

Best Planting Time: Spring

Munro's Globemallow (Sphaeralcea munroana)

Summary: Orange to pink blooms; attracts pollinators.

Care: Well-drained, dry soils. Full sun. Minimal water after establishment.

Insects: Bees and butterflies.

Blooming: Summer

Best Planting Time: Spring or early fall

Arrowleaf Balsamroot (Balsamorhiza sagittata)

Summary: Large yellow flowers like sunflowers; excellent for pollinators.

Care: Rocky, dry soils. Full sun. Very low water once established. Spring blooming.

Insects: Bees, flies, and butterflies.

Blooming: Spring to early summer

Best Planting Time: Fall

Nodding Onion (Allium cernuum)

Summary: Graceful perennial with pink-white flowers; attracts bees.

Care: Well-drained soil. Part sun to full sun. Water only occasionally in summer.

Insects: Bees, flies, and butterflies.

Blooming: Summer (June to August)

Best Planting Time: Fall or spring

Snow Buckwheat (Eriogonum niveum)

Summary: Low grower with delicate white/pink flowers; great for dry gardens.

Care: Rocky or sandy dry soils. Full sun. Low water needs. Late bloom time.

Insects: Bees, flies, and butterflies.

Blooming: Late summer to fall

Best Planting Time: Fall

Strict Buckwheat (Eriogonum strictum)

Summary: Clusters of pink-white flowers; good for rock gardens.

Care: Dry, coarse soil. Full sun. Attracts a variety of pollinators. Minimal watering.

Insects: Bees and butterflies.

Blooming: Summer

Best Planting Time: Fall

Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)

Summary: Tough perennial with lacy foliage and flat flower clusters.

Care: Versatile in dry to moist soils. Full sun. No water needed once established. Can be mowed.

Insects: Wide diversity of insects (such as beetles and wasps).

Blooming: Summer (entire)

Best Planting Time: Fall or spring

Biscuitroot / Desert Parsley (Lomatium aristata)

Summary: Yellow umbel flowers; early bloomer beneficial to native insects.

Care: Dry, rocky soil. Full sun. Extremely drought-tolerant once established.

Insects: Bees, flies, and butterflies.

Blooming: Spring to early summer

Best Planting Time: Fall

Serviceberry (Amelanchier alnifolia)

Summary: Deciduous shrub with white spring flowers and edible berries.

Care: Adaptable to various soils. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation.

Insects: Attracts pollinators in spring.

Blooming: Spring

Best Planting Time: Fall or early spring

Snowberry (Symphoricarpos albus)

Summary: Deciduous shrub with white berries; important wildlife food source.

Care: Adaptable to various soils. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation.

Insects: Attracts pollinators.

Blooming: Summer

Best Planting Time: Fall or early spring

Ninebark (Physocarpus malvaceus)

Summary: Deciduous shrub with attractive peeling bark and white flowers.

Care: Adaptable to various soils. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation.

Insects: Attracts pollinators.

Blooming: Spring/Summer

Best Planting Time: Fall or early spring

Lupines (Lupinus sericeus, L. leucophyllum)

Summary: Perennial with showy pea-like flowers; nitrogen-fixing.

Care: Well-drained soil. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation.

Insects: Attracts bees and butterflies.

Blooming: Spring

Best Planting Time: Fall

Wild Blue Flax (Linum lewisii)

Summary: Delicate blue flowers; drought-tolerant perennial.

Care: Well-drained soil. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation.

Insects: Attracts bees and butterflies.

Blooming: Spring

Best Planting Time: Fall

Bush Penstemon (Penstemon fruticosus)

Summary: Compact shrub with showy flowers; good for rock gardens.

Care: Rocky, well-drained soil. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation.

Insects: Attracts hummingbirds and bees.

Blooming: Spring

Best Planting Time: Fall

Quamash (Camassia quamash)

Summary: Bulb with blue star-shaped flowers; important food source for wildlife.

Care: Well-drained soil. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation. Bulb: short-lived above-ground.

Insects: Attracts bees and butterflies.

Blooming: Spring

Best Planting Time: Fall

Sticky Geranium (Geranium viscosissimum)

Summary: Perennial with pink flowers and sticky stems.

Care: Well-drained soil. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation.

Insects: Attracts bees and butterflies.

Blooming: Summer (entire)

Best Planting Time: Fall or spring

Evening Primrose (Oenothera caespitosa, O. pallida)

Summary: Perennial with white to pink flowers that open in evening.

Care: Rocky, well-drained soil. Part sun to full sun. Water only during water stress in summer, using drip irrigation.

Insects: Attracts night-flying moths.

Blooming: Summer

Best Planting Time: Fall
